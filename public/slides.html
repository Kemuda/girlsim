Why This Game

"If living life as a woman is a game —
what kind of game is it?"

Not a points accumulation game. Not an open world you wander freely.
Something more like: the same map means different things depending on who you are.

So I started asking — what's the worldview? Who are the players?
What are the turns? How do you win? Can you cheat?

"Perception is reality, but reality is also real."

Food for Thought

The Design

Worldview

Structures are real. Agency exists within constraints. Internal life counts as primary data. Nothing is purely individual.

Players

Not demographics — tensions. Adaptor ↔ Challenger. Relational ↔ Individual. Embodied ↔ Transcendent. 8 archetypes that shift.

7 Turns

Girlhood → Threshold → First World → Contraction → Reckoning → Second Wind → Legacy. Between each: a fate card you can't plan for.

Winning

The Integrated Life — you recognize yourself.
The Generous Fire — you gave heat to many.
The Quiet Fortress — you protected something precious.
The Still-Playing — you're still curious. The rarest win.

The only loss: never understanding your own life.

Cheats

Marrying up — works. Coherence penalty in Turn 5.
Suppressing the body — the bill arrives, always.
Performing a character — identity debt accumulates.

Surprise drops

A mentor who sees you clearly.
The right crisis at the right time.
Being witnessed — someone sees you fully.
The second chance — a path reopens.
…

Realizations

Game Design as a Medium

It forces you to make rules about reality

You can't leave things vague. "Agency within constraints" sounds poetic — but when you build it, you have to decide: how much agency? Which constraints? Game design is philosophy with a compiler.

The medium talks back

Writing an essay, you control the reader's path. In a game, the player does things you didn't expect. The design has to be honest enough to survive contact with choice.

Working on Now

The Underlying Rules

An open-world game needs a system underneath — not just scripted scenes.

八字 as Game Engine

The BaZi system (Chinese astrology) gives each character a natal chart — heavenly stems, earthly branches, five elements, ten gods. Not fortune-telling. A constraint generator that makes each playthrough structurally unique.

命 + 运 = 命运

Destiny is given. Luck is chosen. Fate is the product of both. The bazi chart sets your starting conditions; how you play through the 7 turns is yours. This is the core tension the game is built on.

Figuring out the underlying rules of an open-world game — turns out that's the most interesting part.
