Why This Game

"If living life as a woman is a game —
what kind of game is it?"

Not a points accumulation game. Not an open world you wander freely.
Something more like: the same map means different things depending on who you are.

So I started asking — what's the worldview? Who are the players?
What are the turns? How do you win? Can you cheat?

"Perception is reality, but reality is also real."

Food for Thought

The Design

7 turns. Girlhood → Threshold → First World → Contraction → Reckoning → Second Wind → Legacy.
No single win. The Integrated Life. The Generous Fire. The Quiet Fortress. The Still-Playing.
One loss. Never understanding your own life.

Cheats — marrying up, suppressing the body, performing a character. They work. The bill always comes.
Surprise drops — a mentor who sees you clearly. The right crisis at the right time. Being witnessed. …

Realizations

Game Design as a Medium

"Trial and error, reverse-engineering in your mind"

It forces you to make rules about reality. "Agency within constraints" sounds poetic — but when you build it, you have to decide: how much agency? Which constraints? Games teach what schools can't.

"You don't engineer emergence — you discover it"

Simple rules combine into complex life stories you didn't script. You design the sandbox; the player builds something you never imagined.

Working on Now

八字 (BaZi) as Game Engine

A system that generates life rhythms, not storylines.

Why BaZi

Constraint generator — same rules, infinite lives.
相生相克 creates natural drama — no need to script which phase is the climax.
Tracks patterns, not points — endings are mirrors, not scorecards.
No good or bad charts — only intense or balanced.
月柱 defines what each life is about — everyone's wrestling with something different.

How It Works

Draw an era — can't choose where you're born.
Draw a chart — can't choose your gifts.
Luck cycles — can't choose which way the wind blows.
Your choices — the only thing you control.

The ending doesn't judge. It only asks: did you reconcile?

Working on Now

Prototyping Lives

To build a game that rings true, you have to study how life actually plays. Each woman is a test case — what were her constraints? Where did she have agency? What would her parallel universe look like?

Frida Kahlo

Turned body's pain into immortal self-portraits.

Marie Curie

Two Nobel prizes. Cost: radiation, widowhood, exile.

Lila (My Brilliant Friend)

Genius who stayed. Changed everyone. Disappeared.

Jane Goodall

Gave up civilization to understand another species.

Wu Zetian 武则天

Every step a life-or-death gamble.

Hermione Granger (Harry Potter)

Rules keeper who breaks them for love.

Sanmao 三毛

Roamed the world, couldn't escape the dark inside.

Elsa (Frozen)

Suppress your power, or release it — every girl's metaphor.

Chihiro (Spirited Away)

Ordinary girl, thrown into a strange world. Found herself through kindness.
