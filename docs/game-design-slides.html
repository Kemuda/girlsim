What Kind of Game Is It?

Narrative RPG + Ecosystem Simulation

The world isn't open because you can go anywhere — it's open because the same map means different things depending on who you are.

Disco Elysium · Dwarf Fortress · Civilization · Tarot / I Ching

"Perception is reality, but reality is also real."

Worldview / Game Philosophy

01

Structures exist and matter — society, class, culture are real forces

02

Agency exists within constraints — you can't escape the board, but you can play it differently

03

Internal life is primary data — how you feel matters as much as the outcome

04

Nothing is purely individual — your moves affect others invisibly

05

Time is nonlinear — one event at 14 reshapes meaning at 40

"The game doesn't moralize. It observes. It reflects."

Early Game

Girlhood (0–12)

You receive. Passive absorption — family scripts, body messages, early relational data. Limited agency, maximum impressionability.

The Threshold (12–18)

First major divergence. Social systems fully activate. Sexuality, ambition, belonging — first real choices with lasting weight.

First World (18–28)

Peak expansion. Maximum optionality, minimum wisdom. The game feels most like an open world here. Risk/reward ratios are extreme.

Mid Game

The Contraction (28–38)

Doors begin closing. But closing a door isn't losing — sometimes it's building a room. Trade-off intelligence becomes core skill.

Midgame Reckoning (38–50)

Inventory audit. What did I accumulate? What did I give away? The game's most emotionally complex phase.

Late Game

Second Wind or Unraveling (50–65)

Bifurcation point. Players who built internal resources experience unexpected expansion. Others face the cost of deferred choices.

Legacy Turn (65+)

The game shifts to what you generate in others. Your primary resource becomes influence, story, and transmission.

Between every turn: a Threshold Event — an unexpected card that can't be planned for.

Winning Scenarios

There is no single winning scenario.

1. The Integrated Life
Your internal self and external life are legible to each other. You recognize yourself.
2. The Generous Fire
You burned intensely and gave heat to many. You may be depleted, but the world is measurably warmer.
3. The Quiet Fortress
You protected something precious — a child, a craft, a community, a self — against enormous pressure.
4. The Late Bloomer
You arrive at something essential in Turn 6 or 7 that others found in Turn 2. The timing was right for you.
5. The Honest Failure
You didn't reach success, but you understood why, made peace, and transmitted that knowledge. This counts.
6. The Still-Playing
Endgame arrives and you are still genuinely curious. Nothing is closed. Perhaps the rarest win.
"Dying having never understood your own life is the only true loss state. And even then, the game asks: was it the player, or the conditions?"

Cheats & Shortcuts

Cheats (work, with cost)

  • Marrying up — trades internal coherence; penalty surfaces in Turn 5
  • Suppressing the body — temporarily increases output; the bill arrives, always
  • Performing a character — effective short-term; identity debt accumulates

Shortcuts (genuine, rare)

  • A mentor who sees you clearly — compresses 10 years into 2
  • The right crisis at the right time — suffering with support = fastest growth
  • Finding your medium early — everything compounds from Turn 2–3

Surprise Packages

Random drops, can't be earned.

The unexpected friendship — changes your resource map entirely
The body that heals — resilience beyond model
The stranger who tells you the truth — a single sentence reorganizes years
The second chance — a path reopens; rarer after Turn 4
Being witnessed — someone sees you fully, at the right moment. Exceedingly rare.

Hidden Mechanics

The Mirror System

Your choices create ripples for other women in your network. The game tracks this, but doesn't show the score until very late. How do others experience your turn?

The Unchosen Lives

Every branching decision generates a ghost — a parallel path not taken. Visible occasionally in Turn 5. They're not there to haunt you. They're data.

The Body & Grief

Grief as Mechanic

Not penalty — a processing phase. A necessary metabolism. Skipping it stores unprocessed material as weight that accumulates. Those who grieve well, move well.

The Body as Co-Player

Not your avatar — an NPC with its own agenda you're in constant negotiation with. It gives information. It overrides you. Learning to read it is one of the highest-skill activities.

The Game Knows You're Playing

"The more self-aware your character becomes about the game's structures, the more agency she has — and the more she can see the arbitrariness of the rules."
